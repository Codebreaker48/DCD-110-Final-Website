This Week in NASCAR - Bristol

TY GIBBS GETS IT DONE!!!

A driver that we have been talking about all season and even before, Ty Gibbs finally prevails for the firtst win of his career. The season of toyota continues as another strong performance come sfor their best drivers. JGR drivers Hamln and Bell were also fast all weekedn, but it was Gibbs who prevailed in the end

Stage 1 was classic Bristol, all beating and banging, but some amazing racing all throughout the pack. We saw runs off the top and the bottom, which created an incredible spectacle as anyone and everyone was moving throught he field. As always, track position was the most important thing. The leaders were able to pull away and lap a majority of the field before the end of the stage.

Stage 2 saw a lot of the same, but it also had a much larger emphasis on the strategy. Tire wear began to set in during the long green flag runs and it created a shift in the front. Drivers like Kyle Larson who was able to dirve away early in the race began to fall back. This became a problem for many other drivers, but the pit startegy brought them right back into it. The toyotas continued to dominate.

Stage 3 saw the energy pick up significantly. As we have come to expect from Bristol. The pushes got harder and no one was looking to consevre the car. One of these pushes caused a massive accident with a few big contenders involved. Pit stops afte this shuffled up the field allowing Ty Gibbs to make a charge towards the fornt. After an intense battle with his teamate Denny Hamlin, he was able to hold on and grab his first career win

Gibbs is the grandson of the legendary team owner Joe Gibbs. He has been heavily scouted as one of the best drivers for the future of the sport, but today he proved that he is actually one of the best drivers period. He has come close many times, but has always been beaten out by his teamates. But not today. The toyota dominance continues, but this time it was the rookie getting in on the fun. Lets see if he can keep it up as we go to another 1.5-mile track in Kansas.
