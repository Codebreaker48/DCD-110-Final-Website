This Week in NASCAR - Darlington

Reddick notches number 4!

During throwback week at Darlington, we were gifted with some amazing paint schemes, but we got an even better race. In the end, Tyler Reddick would get his fourth win of the season after an incredible comeback after some early setbacks. We also saw some great runs from the RFK fords in honor of the Biff.

In stage 1, it proved to be a very track position heavy race. Darlington is known as a very technical track. Tire wear is a very important thing so we didnt see a lot of pushing in the middle of the field. Reddick ran away with it from the pole, but Ryan Blaney and the RFK fords began running him down. After a mistake on pit road, Reddick would los spots allowing Brad Keselowski to win the stage.

Stage 2 saw most of the same stuff. Lots of conserving tires and taking care of your stuff. Restarts continued to be wild, but there was very few incidents or moments of contact. Reddick had another issue that led him down pit road, bringing him a lap down. After a solid stage, Brad K escpaed with another stage win

Stage 3 saw the race hit a new level. Brad K and the RFK fords continued to dominate. Pit stops and cautions kept bunching the field up, but most importantly, it allowed Tyler Reddick to work his way back to the lead lap. After facing power issues early in the race, Reddick made an incredible run to take the lead with 5 laps to go. That was all she wrote.

Reddick claims his fourth win in just 6 races. Simply unseen in recent NASCAR history, but you have to respect what this team is doing. The toyotas are dominating this season and the 45 is at the helm. Can they continue the dominance and we go to the first 2 short tracks of the season?
