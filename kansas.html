This Week in NASCAR - Kansas

REDDICK GETS NUMBER 5!!

The Cup series returned to Kansas Speedway this weekend in and delivered! After some late race CHAOS, Tyler Reddick was able to make an amazing run to solidify the victory! It was his fifth of the season in just 9 races. Something truly difficult to believe at this point, but it's true. The man who won ZERO races last season has won more than half of the races through 9 weeks.

In stage 1, it was clear that clean air was going to be a big thing for the leaders. There was very little action up front and even further back in the field. There were lots of small battles and drivers who were making moves, but nothing to write home about by any means.

In Stage 2, we started to see a bit more action. The long green flag runs led to more tire wear throughout the field. We got green flag pit stops and lots of passes, though most of them were not battles, but just quick moves. It was a true showcase of strategy

Stage 3 showed much of the same. The drivers at the front were able to maintain their places, but the strategy and tire wear played a massive role as always. It looked like a full-on fight between Kyle Larson, Denny Hamlin, and Tyler Reddick. Reddick had the lead but would make a mistake by getting into the wall, which allowed Denny Hamlin to take the lead. It looked like he would run away with the win, but Cody Ware had other plans as he spun to bring out the first caution for the cause of the day.

All the drama would bump up in overtime as Hamlin and Larson would make contact, allowing Reddick to pounce. He wouldn't miss that opportunity, and he would go on to win. The dominance that the 45 teams have shown is undeniable. It's almost unbelievable, but we are seeing it right before our eyes. Lets see if her can keep it up going into Talladega. A track he has won at before!
