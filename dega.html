This Week in NASCAR - Talladega

WE HAVE A FIRST TIME WINNER!!!

After talking about it all year, Carson Hocevar has finally gotten his first win of his Cup Series career. In just the third Super Speedway race of the season, we got an absolute thriller. While it wasn't something that many fans would consider to be the greatest race ever, it certainly delivered.

Stage 1 was filled with fuel saving. NASCAR attempted to change the races by making the first stage longer than the others, but I don't think that it hit the mark that they were looking for. However, it certainly provided a lot of strategy and some tight racing during the stage.

Stage 2 is where everything fell apart. With the stage system setup, this stage was much shorter, meaning that there was much more aggressive pushing. About 20 laps in, Bubba Wallace would get a bad push while leading a 3 wide field. He shot into the outside wall, and chaos unfolded behind him. The crash that followed caused damage to 26 cars in the field. Only about 10 got away completely unscathed. These included many big-time contenders in the race. And after this moment, the race completely changed.

Stage 3 was led by Carson Hocevar and Chris Buescher. There were almost always two lines, and it was a constant back and forth. It was a truly exciting watch, especially if your driver was towards the front. In the end, however, the top line would fall apart, and Alex Bowman would deliver the final push to the bumper of Carson Hocevar to solidify his first win!

The celebration that followed will go down in NASCAR history as Carson would drive the car while sitting on the window frame. Celebrating with the fans and even trying to catch a beer being thrown at him while driving down the frontstretch. Certainly a great moment as we prepare for Texas next week!
