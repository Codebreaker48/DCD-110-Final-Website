This Week in NASCAR - Martinsville

CHEVY IS ON THE BOARD!

After an awful start to the season (by Chevy Standards), the bowties finally got their first win of the season. No suprise that it came at Martinsville, one of the best treacks for Hendrick Motorsports. Chase was running upfront all day and was able to capitazile late in the race

In stage 1, we saw all classic martinsville. It was all beating and banging during the start, but eventually settled down. Not a lot of passing as you would expect form a short track like this. However, there was plenty of action in the field. The classic drivers like Denny Hamlin were all dominating the field and that wouldnt change.

Stage 2 was much of the same, we didnt see many accidents, but tire wear was becoming an issue. Pit road stategy would take over the race and completely shake up the field. At a track like martinsville, any mistake can cost you multiple positions or even lape. This was even more true for Kyle larson. Leading upfront all day, he would hav an issue of pit road and it was downhill from there. The main 5 upfront stayed the seame

In stage 3, we saw a lot of the same stuff. Lots of pit strategy and extra work to do for a lot of drivers. But cautions and pitstops would truly decide the race. After a late race caution, the leaders owuld come down pit road, and after laying back all day, Chase Elliott would pounce with an amazing pit stop putting him into the lead. the last restart saw Denny Hamlin get shuffled back, but he would make a final push. However it wouldnt be enough and Chase Elliott would hold on to get his first win of the year.

It has been a tough season for the 9 team in terms of race winning speed, but today they were able to get it done. This propelled the 9 into the top 5 in points and provided the whole team with a much needed confidence boost. The only questions whether or not they can keep it up going into Bristol.
